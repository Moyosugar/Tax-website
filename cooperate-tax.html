What is Corporate Revenue Tax?

Corporations and other business entities, such as partnerships and limited liability companies (LLCs), have distinct tax obligations. They are typically subject to corporate income tax on their profits. The tax rates and filing requirements for corporations may differ from those for individual taxpayers. Some countries also impose additional taxes, such as franchise taxes or minimum business taxes, on certain types of businesses......load more

The due date for filing returns

  • For newly incorporated companies, within eighteen (18) months from the date of incorporation or not later than six (6) months after the end of its accounting period, whichever is earlier
  • For newly incorporated companies, within eighteen (18) months from the date of incorporation or not later than six (6) months after the end of its accounting period, whichever is earlier For existing companies, within six (6) months from the end of the accounting year.
  • A self- assessment filer may (by application) commence payment of instalment before due date but such instalment cannot extend beyond two months after the due date
  • Companies in operation for more than four (4) years are liable to minimum tax except those specifically exempted by the tax law

Minimum Tax under CITA arises where

  • A company makes a loss
  • A company has no tax payable
  • A self- assessment filer may (by application) commence payment of instalment before due date but such instalment cannot extend beyond two months after the due date
  • Tax payable is less than minimum tax​

Individual

Individual taxpayers are individuals who earn income through employment, self-employment, investments, or other sources. They are responsible for paying income tax on their earnings, and the tax liability is determined based on the progressive tax rates set by the government. Individual taxpayers are typically required to file annual tax returns, report their income, deductions, and claim applicable tax credits or exemptions.

Corporate

Corporations and other business entities, such as partnerships and limited liability companies (LLCs), have distinct tax obligations. They are typically subject to corporate income tax on their profits. The tax rates and filing requirements for corporations may differ from those for individual taxpayers. Some countries also impose additional taxes, such as franchise taxes or minimum business taxes, on certain types of businesses.
