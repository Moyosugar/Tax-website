What is the individual revenue tax?

Individual taxpayers are individuals who earn income through employment, self-employment, investments, or other sources. They are responsible for paying income tax on their earnings, and the tax liability is determined based on the progressive tax rates set by the government. Individual taxpayers are typically required to file annual tax returns, report their income, and deductions, and claim applicable tax credits or exemptions.

Individual

Individual taxpayers are typically required to file annual tax returns, report their income, deductions, and claim applicable tax credits or exemptions.

Corporate

Corporations and other business entities, such as partnerships and limited liability companies (LLCs), have distinct tax obligations. They are typically subject to corporate income tax on their profits. The tax rates and filing requirements for corporations may differ from those for individual taxpayers. Some countries also impose additional taxes, such as franchise taxes or minimum business taxes, on certain types of businesses.
